# Chapter 10 — Templates and checklists

Copy these per opportunity and fill them in. They are deliberately lightweight, the
point is to force the right thinking, not to generate paperwork. All are Assessment
(working tools), adapt freely to your organization.

## Bid / no-bid scorecard

cannot cite evidence, the real score is low.

## Capture plan (one-pager core)

- **Opportunity:** name, agency/PEO/PM, vehicle, est. value, RFP and award dates.
- **Mission gap / customer pain:** the problem in their words.
- **Stakeholder map:** end user, PM/requirements owner, KO, COR, SSA, budget,
  testers, with names and our relationship status for each.
- **Incumbent and competitors:** who, their likely approach, their CPARS standing.
- **Our discriminators (2-4):** each tested for customer value, truth, and
  uniqueness.
- **Win themes:** need → discriminator → proof → benefit, one per theme.
- **Teaming:** prime/sub roles, teammates, workshare, TA/NDA status.
- **Price-to-win:** target price/range, basis, our cost posture, gap to close.
- **Shaping actions:** RFIs, white papers, demos, one-on-ones, with owners/dates.
- **Gap-closure plan:** every weakness, with owner and due date.
- **PWin now / PWin target / next gate date.**

## Customer call plan / call report

Before the call:
- Who we are meeting (name, role, power) and our objective for this contact.
- The two or three things we want to learn.
- The one credibility point we want to demonstrate (not assert).

After the call (the call report):
- What we learned (priorities, constraints, view of incumbent, timeline).
- Their concerns or objections.
- Commitments made (ours and theirs).
- Next action, owner, and date. Update PWin and the capture plan.

## Compliance matrix (per proposal)

| ID | Source (L/M/C ref) | Requirement ("shall" / instruction / factor) | Proposal section | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | | |

Decompose every instruction (Section L), evaluation factor (Section M), and "shall"
(Section C/SOW/PWS) into its own row. Nothing ships until every row is green.

## Color-team review checklist

- **Pink (early draft):** compliant to L/M? solution sound? win themes present?
  structure right? Course-correct now.
- **Red (near-final):** score it as the evaluator would, against Section M.
  Compliant, compelling, would it win? Findings drive the final rewrite.
- **Gold (final):** executive approval to submit, price included.
- **White-glove:** format, page limits, cross-references, file types, portal,
  deadline. Confirm receipt after submission.

## Post-decision / debrief capture

- Outcome and award amount; winner if known.
- Our scored strengths, weaknesses, significant weaknesses, deficiencies (from the
  debrief).
- Evaluator quotes and the discriminators that did or did not land.
- Price read: where were we vs. award?
- Lessons for the next pursuit with this customer.
- Protest assessment: are there grounds, and is it worth the cost and the
  relationship? (See [chapter 3](03-acquisition-landscape.md).)
