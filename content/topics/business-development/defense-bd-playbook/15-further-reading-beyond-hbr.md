# Chapter 15 — Further reading beyond HBR

HBR ([chapter 14](14-further-reading-hbr.md)) is one lane. This chapter collects
the other authoritative sources for defense BD: the practitioner guides, the
consulting and think-tank analysis, the government ground truth, and the classic
books. Assessment: recommendations are based on titles, public summaries, and
publisher reputation; treat them as a shortlist, not a verified synthesis. For
ongoing monitoring (feeds, alerts, where to watch week to week) see
[chapter 13 (staying current)](13-staying-current.md); this chapter is the deeper
readings to learn from.

## Practitioner craft (start here)
The most directly actionable, because they are the frameworks this playbook is
built on.

- Shipley Associates, [Business Development Lifecycle Guide, Capture Guide, and
  Proposal Guide](https://www.shipleywins.com/products) (Larry Newman). The
  canonical practitioner references: a 7-phase, 96-step BD lifecycle, plus
  dedicated capture and proposal guides. The Proposal Guide is the basis for APMP
  certification. Paid books. These underpin
  [chapters 1](01-capture-lifecycle.md), [6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md), and
  [7](07-proposal-management.md).
- APMP, [Body of Knowledge and certification program](https://www.apmp.org/certification/)
  (Association of Proposal Management Professionals). Vendor-neutral best practice
  for bid, proposal, and capture work; the Foundation study glossary is free, the
  full BoK and certs need membership. Reinforces
  [chapters 2](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md) and
  [7](07-proposal-management.md).

## Consulting and industry analysis
Mostly free to read online. Good for market context, customer priorities, and the
direction of acquisition reform (useful for shaping, [chapter 4](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md)).

- McKinsey, [Aerospace & Defense insights](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/aerospace-and-defense/our-insights),
  notably [International defense sales: Opportunities, but not for all](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/aerospace-and-defense/our-insights/international-defense-sales)
  and [Strengthening America's defense industry](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/public-sector/our-insights/strengthening-american-defense-industry).
- Deloitte, [2026 Aerospace and Defense Industry Outlook](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/aerospace-defense/aerospace-and-defense-industry-outlook.html).
- PwC, [Aerospace and defense industry trends](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/industries/industrial-products/library/aerospace-and-defense-trends.html)
  and the A&D deals/M&A outlook.
- Assessment: a recurring theme across all three is the shift from cost-plus to
  outcome-based contracting and the rise of defense-tech entrants, both of which
  bear on price-to-win ([chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md)) and the
  acquisition landscape ([chapter 3](03-acquisition-landscape.md)).

## Government and oversight (ground truth)
Free and authoritative. Cite these, not the commentary, when a fact has to be
right.

- DAU, [Adaptive Acquisition Framework](https://aaf.dau.edu/aaf/aaf-pathways/)
  (Defense Acquisition University). The six acquisition pathways (urgent, middle
  tier, major capability, software, business systems, services); essential context
  for [chapter 3](03-acquisition-landscape.md).
- GAO, [Bid Protest Annual Report to Congress](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-26-900695)
  and [Bid Protests: Key Features and Trends](https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-108652/index.html).
  The real numbers: a sustain rate around 16 percent and an effectiveness rate
  around 52 percent in recent years. Grounds the protest discussion in
  [chapter 3](03-acquisition-landscape.md).
- Also worth pulling directly: the DoD Source Selection Procedures guide, and CRS
  (Congressional Research Service) reports on defense acquisition and OTAs.

## Think tanks and market intelligence
- CSIS Defense-Industrial Initiatives Group, [DoD Contracts Report](https://www.csis.org/programs/defense-industrial-initiatives-group/dod-contracts-report),
  the annual [Defense Acquisition Trends](https://www.csis.org/analysis/defense-acquisition-trends-2023-preliminary-look),
  and [DoD Other Transaction Authority Trends](https://www.csis.org/analysis/department-defense-other-transaction-authority-trends-new-rd-funding-paradigm).
  The best public read on who DoD actually buys from, how concentrated it is among
  the Big Five, how much goes uncompeted, and how fast OTAs are growing. Directly
  feeds competitive analysis ([chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md)).
- RAND (acquisition and technology policy research) and Govini (the National
  Security Scorecard) are also worth watching for data-driven analysis. Govini is
  largely a paid platform.

## Books (the relationship and win side)
The playbook compresses these; the originals go deeper. All paid, widely available.

- Neil Rackham, *SPIN Selling*. The research-backed consultative-discovery method;
  pairs with the call plan in [chapter 4](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md).
- Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson, *The Challenger Sale*. Teaching and reframing the
  customer's view; useful for shaping and win themes.
- Robert Miller & Stephen Heiman, *The New Strategic Selling*. Mapping the
  multi-stakeholder buying organization, the commercial analogue of the defense
  stakeholder map.
- Tom Sant, *Persuasive Business Proposals*. A proposal-writing classic for
  [chapter 7](07-proposal-management.md).
- Roger Fisher & William Ury, *Getting to Yes*, and David Lax & James Sebenius,
  *3-D Negotiation*. The negotiation foundations behind
  [chapter 5](05-teaming-partnerships.md) and the campaign framing in
  [chapter 4](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md). (3-D Negotiation is the book
  behind the "Deal Making 2.0" article in [chapter 14](14-further-reading-hbr.md).)

## How to use this list
Assessment: if you read only three things, make them the Shipley Capture Guide (the
craft), the latest CSIS DoD Contracts Report (the market), and the GAO Bid Protest
Annual Report (the risk). Everything else is depth on a specific chapter. When a
source changes how the playbook should read, paste the passage and I will
synthesize it in, with honest labels per the vault's rules.
