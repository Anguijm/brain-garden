# Chapter 9 — Pipeline, metrics, and cadence

BD that is not measured drifts into busywork and wishful pipelines. Assessment: run
BD as a managed system with a visible pipeline, a few honest metrics, and a
recurring battle rhythm, the same way a program is run.

## The pipeline

Maintain a single pipeline of opportunities, each with a stage, an estimated value,
an expected RFP and award date, the contract vehicle, the incumbent, the assigned
capture manager, and a current PWin. Assessment: stages should mirror the lifecycle
in [chapter 1](01-capture-lifecycle.md) and the gates in
[chapter 2](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md):

1. Identified (raw lead, on strategy)
2. Qualified (passed Gate 1, capture assigned)
3. In capture (shaping and planning underway)
4. Bid (passed Gate 3, in proposal)
5. Submitted (awaiting decision)
6. Won / Lost (with debrief captured)

## Weighted pipeline value

Assessment: multiply each opportunity's value by its PWin to get a weighted, more
honest pipeline figure, and sum it by stage and by expected award date. This
exposes two classic problems: a pipeline that looks huge but is mostly low-PWin
hope, and a coverage gap where not enough qualified value is moving toward award in
the quarters ahead. As a rough planning heuristic, many shops want several
multiples of their revenue target in qualified pipeline, because most pursuits do
not convert.

## Metrics worth watching

Assessment: track a small set and act on them, rather than a dashboard nobody uses.

- **Win rate** (wins divided by decisions), and separately for new work vs.
  recompetes, since recompetes should win far more often.
- **Submit rate / no-bid discipline:** are you killing weak pursuits early, or
  bidding everything? A very high bid rate often signals weak qualification.
- **Capture cycle time:** how long from qualified to award, so you can plan B&P.
- **B&P return:** revenue won per B&P dollar spent, the blunt measure of whether
  your BD investment pays.
- **Pipeline coverage:** weighted qualified value vs. target, by period.
- **PWin accuracy:** compare predicted PWin to actual outcomes over time, to catch
  systematic optimism.

Assessment: the single most important behavior these metrics should drive is
honesty, especially about PWin and no-bids. Metrics that punish people for honest
no-bids will produce dishonest pipelines.

## The battle rhythm

Assessment: a predictable cadence keeps the system alive. A workable rhythm:

- **Weekly:** capture stand-ups on active pursuits, what moved, what is blocked,
  next actions on the call plan and gap-closure list.
- **Monthly:** pipeline review with leadership, stage changes, new qualifications,
  PWin updates, gate decisions due.
- **Quarterly:** portfolio and strategy review, are we on the right customers and
  vehicles, what is our coverage for the next several quarters, where do we need to
  invest or divest.
- **Per opportunity:** the gate reviews from [chapter 2](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md),
  held when the pursuit is ready, not on a fixed date.
- **Always:** an after-action review on every win and loss, fed by the debrief, so
  the organization actually learns.

## The point of the system

Assessment: the pipeline and metrics are not paperwork, they are how you make sure
scarce capture and B&P resources are aimed at the few pursuits you can win. A
smaller pipeline of well-qualified, well-shaped opportunities at high PWin beats a
sprawling one chased thin, every time.
