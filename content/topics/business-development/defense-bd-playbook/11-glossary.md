# Chapter 11 — Glossary

The acronym soup, decoded. Definitions are concise working ones; for regulatory
terms the controlling source is the FAR/DFARS.

- **B&P** — Bid and Proposal. The (often unrecovered) money you spend pursuing and
  proposing work. Finite, which is why qualification matters.
- **Black Hat** — A session role-playing competitors to predict their bids and find
  where you are exposed. See [chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).
- **BPA** — Blanket Purchase Agreement. A streamlined arrangement for recurring
  purchases, often off a GSA Schedule.
- **Capture** — The pre-RFP campaign to make a possible opportunity into a winnable
  one. The heart of this playbook.
- **CO / KO** — Contracting Officer. The only person who can legally bind the
  government. (KO is the common spoken/written shorthand.)
- **COR** — Contracting Officer's Representative. The KO's technical eyes on
  contract performance.
- **CPARS** — Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System. Where your
  performance is graded; those grades become your past-performance record.
- **DFARS** — Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement. The DoD layer on
  top of the FAR.
- **Discriminator** — Something you do that the customer values, that is true, and
  that competitors cannot credibly claim.
- **EN** — Evaluation Notice. The government's written question/issue during
  evaluation or discussions.
- **FAR** — Federal Acquisition Regulation. The government-wide procurement
  rulebook.
- **FFP / CPFF / CPIF / T&M** — Contract types: Firm-Fixed-Price; Cost-Plus-Fixed-
  Fee; Cost-Plus-Incentive-Fee; Time-and-Materials. See
  [chapter 3](03-acquisition-landscape.md).
- **FPR** — Final Proposal Revision. Your last submission after discussions.
- **GAO** — Government Accountability Office. Runs the independent bid-protest
  forum.
- **GWAC** — Government-Wide Acquisition Contract. A pre-competed, government-wide
  vehicle (typically IT).
- **IDIQ** — Indefinite-Delivery, Indefinite-Quantity. Umbrella contract under
  which task/delivery orders are competed.
- **LPTA** — Lowest-Price Technically Acceptable. Evaluation where technical is
  pass/fail and lowest acceptable price wins (FAR 15.101-2).
- **MEDDPICC** — A qualification checklist (Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision
  criteria, Decision process, Paper process, Identify pain, Champion, Competition).
  Adapted for govcon in [chapter 2](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md).
- **NDA** — Non-Disclosure Agreement. Sign before exchanging sensitive pursuit
  information.
- **OCI** — Organizational Conflict of Interest (FAR Subpart 9.5). Unequal access,
  biased ground rules, or impaired objectivity. See
  [chapter 8](08-relationships-ethics-oci.md).
- **OT / OTA** — Other Transaction / Other Transaction Authority. Non-FAR
  agreements for research (10 U.S.C. 4021) and prototypes/production (10 U.S.C.
  4022).
- **Past performance** — Your record on prior, relevant contracts; a major
  evaluation factor and a reason to sub before you prime.
- **PEO** — Program Executive Office. The portfolio layer above individual program
  managers.
- **PM** — Program Manager (government side). Owns the program and shapes the
  requirement.
- **PTW** — Price-to-Win. The competitor- and customer-driven target price at which
  you would most likely win and still perform. See
  [chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).
- **PWin** — Probability of Win. Your honest, re-scored estimate of winning. See
  [chapter 2](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md).
- **PWS / SOW / SOO** — Performance Work Statement / Statement of Work / Statement
  of Objectives. What the government wants done (SOO states objectives and lets you
  propose the how).
- **RFI** — Request for Information. Pre-solicitation market research you should
  answer to shape the requirement.
- **RFP / RFQ** — Request for Proposal / Request for Quotation. The solicitation.
- **SAT** — Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($350,000 as of Oct 1, 2025; verify
  current). Below it, simplified procedures and small-business reservation apply.
- **Section L / Section M** — Solicitation instructions (L) and evaluation factors
  (M). Write to M, comply with L. See [chapter 7](07-proposal-management.md).
- **Set-aside** — A competition reserved for small or specific socioeconomic
  businesses (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB) under FAR Part 19.
- **Shaping** — Legally influencing the customer's understanding and the
  requirement before the RFP locks. See
  [chapter 4](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md).
- **SBIR / STTR** — Small Business Innovation Research / Small Business Technology
  Transfer. Phased innovation programs; Phase III can be sole-sourced.
- **SSA** — Source Selection Authority. The official who makes the award decision.
- **TA** — Teaming Agreement. Pre-award statement of intended prime/sub roles and
  workshare. See [chapter 5](05-teaming-partnerships.md).
- **Win theme** — A repeated, evidence-backed message tying a customer need to a
  discriminator and a benefit.
