# Chapter 14 — Further reading (HBR)

A curated reading list from Harvard Business Review, organized by the playbook
chapter each piece reinforces. Assessment: these recommendations are based on the
public titles and summaries, not the full articles (the bodies are behind HBR's
paywall), so treat the list as a shortlist worth your subscription time rather than
a verified synthesis. When a piece lands, paste the passages you want and I will
fold them into the relevant chapter.

A note on the links: most go to subscription articles on hbr.org. A few are HBR
Store products (case studies or reprints sold separately); those are flagged
"[HBR Store]".

## Decision quality, PWin, and avoiding overconfidence
For [chapter 2 (qualification, PWin, gates)](02-qualification-pwin-gates.md) and
[chapter 10 (Gen AI)](10-gen-ai-in-bd.md). This is the strongest cluster for BD,
because PWin, bid/no-bid, and price-to-win are all forecasts under uncertainty.

- Kahneman, Lovallo & Sibony, [The Big Idea: Before You Make That Big Decision…](https://hbr.org/2011/06/the-big-idea-before-you-make-that-big-decision)
  (2011). A 12-question checklist to catch bias in a team's recommendation. Read it
  as a template for tightening your gate reviews and bid/no-bid decisions.
- Lovallo & Kahneman, [Delusions of Success: How Optimism Undermines Executives' Decisions](https://hbr.org/2003/07/delusions-of-success-how-optimism-undermines-executives-decisions)
  (2003). Introduces the "outside view" and reference-class forecasting: estimate
  PWin from the actual win rate of your class of similar past pursuits, not an
  inside-view gut feel. Also names "competitor neglect," which is the bias a Black
  Hat ([chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md)) exists to fix.
- Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein, [Noise](https://hbr.org/2016/10/noise) (2016).
  "Decision hygiene" against inconsistent judgment. Useful for making PWin and
  bid/no-bid scoring repeatable across capture managers.
- [Why Smart People (Sometimes) Make Bad Decisions](https://hbr.org/podcast/2021/05/why-smart-people-sometimes-make-bad-decisions)
  (2021, podcast). An accessible companion to the above.

## Win strategy and differentiation
For [chapter 6 (win strategy and competitive analysis)](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).

- [Discovering New Points of Differentiation](https://hbr.org/1997/07/discovering-new-points-of-differentiation)
  (1997). Differentiate at every customer touchpoint, not just the product. Feeds
  the discriminator hunt.
- [You Can Win Without Differentiation](https://hbr.org/2013/10/you-can-win-without-differentiation)
  (2013). A contrarian read, useful for commodity-like or LPTA buys.
- [The Secret to Becoming a Market Leader](https://hbr.org/2020/12/the-secret-to-becoming-a-market-leader)
  (2020). Target the customers you want rather than being customer-led, which is
  the portfolio logic of [chapter 1, phase 0](01-capture-lifecycle.md).

## Persuasion, storytelling, proposals, and orals
For [chapter 7 (proposal management)](07-proposal-management.md), and win themes in
[chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).

- [The Art of Persuasion Hasn't Changed in 2,000 Years](https://hbr.org/2019/07/the-art-of-persuasion-hasnt-changed-in-2000-years)
  (2019). Ethos, pathos, logos: a clean structure for win themes.
- [A Great Sales Pitch Hinges on the Right Story](https://hbr.org/2024/05/a-great-sales-pitch-hinges-on-the-right-story)
  (2024) and [Using Stories to Persuade](https://hbr.org/2011/03/using-stories-as-a-tool-of-per)
  (2011). Frame the customer's mission gap as the story, with your solution as the
  resolution.
- [How to Give a Killer Presentation](https://hbr.org/2013/06/how-to-give-a-killer-presentation)
  (2013). For oral presentations.
- [6 Pitching Techniques to Use When Budgets Are Tight](https://hbr.org/2023/05/6-pitching-techniques-to-use-when-budgets-are-tight)
  (2023). Apt for cost-constrained and LPTA bids.

## Negotiation (teaming and price-to-win)
For [chapter 5 (teaming)](05-teaming-partnerships.md) and price-to-win in
[chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).

- [Deal Making 2.0: A Guide to Complex Negotiations](https://hbr.org/2012/11/deal-making-20-a-guide-to-complex-negotiations)
  (Lax & Sebenius, 2012). Multi-front "negotiation campaigns" across many parties,
  which maps onto the diffuse defense buyer and multi-party teaming. Its six-step
  campaign framework is now synthesized into
  [chapter 4](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md) ("Run capture as a negotiation
  campaign").
- [What's Your Negotiation Strategy?](https://hbr.org/2020/07/whats-your-negotiation-strategy)
  (2020).
- [Negotiating with a Customer You Can't Afford to Lose](https://hbr.org/1988/11/negotiating-with-a-customer-you-cant-afford-to-lose)
  (1988). A classic, apt for recompetes and incumbency.
- [How Sales Teams Undercut Themselves with Longtime Clients](https://hbr.org/2026/05/how-sales-teams-undercut-themselves-with-longtime-clients)
  (2026). Premature concessions with incumbents, sharp for recompete pricing.

## Teaming and alliances
For [chapter 5 (teaming and partnerships)](05-teaming-partnerships.md).

- [Simple Rules for Making Alliances Work](https://hbr.org/2007/11/simple-rules-for-making-alliances-work)
  (2007).
- [Is Your Strategic Alliance Really a Sale?](https://hbr.org/1995/01/is-your-strategic-alliance-really-a-sale)
  (1995). A useful lens on prime/sub power dynamics.
- [Research: Joint Ventures that Keep Evolving Perform Better](https://hbr.org/2021/04/research-joint-ventures-that-keep-evolving-perform-better)
  (2021).
- *Strategic Alliances That Work: Selecting the Right Partner* and *Strategic
  Alliances: Three Ways to Make Them Work* [HBR Store] — partner-selection case
  material if you want depth.

## Pricing and price-to-win
For price-to-win in [chapter 6](06-win-strategy-competitive.md).

- [A Quick Guide to Value-Based Pricing](https://hbr.org/2016/08/a-quick-guide-to-value-based-pricing)
  (2016).
- [How Do You Know When the Price Is Right?](https://hbr.org/1995/09/how-do-you-know-when-the-price-is-right)
  (1995). A classic on pricing judgment.

## Customer relationships and stakeholder management
For [chapter 4 (customer, stakeholders, shaping)](04-customer-stakeholders-shaping.md)
and [chapter 8 (relationships)](08-relationships-ethics-oci.md).

- [How to Succeed at Key Account Management](https://hbr.org/2012/07/how-to-succeed-at-key-account)
  (2012). Team-based account relationships, mirroring the stakeholder map and call
  plan.
- [Build Customer Relationships That Last](https://hbr.org/1985/11/build-customer-relationships-that-last)
  (1985). A classic on relationship-building as the long game.
- *ABB and Caterpillar (A): Key Account Management* and *Siemens AG: Key Account
  Management* [HBR Store] — case studies if you want worked examples.

## How to use this list
Assessment: read against a specific live pursuit, not in the abstract. Pick the
chapter you are working (say, building the capture plan), pull the two or three
pieces mapped to it, and mine them for one idea you can apply this week. When you
find something that should change the playbook itself, paste it in and I will
synthesize and re-publish, with honest labels per the vault's rules.
