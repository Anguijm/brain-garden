# Chapter 12 — Arranging

A loop is not a song. Arrangement is how you go from one good bar to an intro, verse,
chorus, and outro. On the K.O. II that is built on three ideas: patterns, scenes,
and commit.

## Patterns within a group

FACT: Each group holds up to 99 patterns. Switch the active pattern for a group by
holding that group button (`GROUP A`-`D`) and pressing `-` / `+`, or holding the
group and typing the number on the pads. Because each group has its own active
pattern, you "mix and match" patterns across groups to create variations on the fly.

## Commit: the core workflow

FACT: `SHIFT` + `MAIN` is **commit**. It snapshots the current combination of
patterns into a **scene** and moves you into a fresh copy, so you can keep
layering and varying without overwriting what you just made and without stopping the
music. This is the heartbeat of building a track on this device: get a loop you
like, commit, change something, commit again.

Assessment: think of commit as "save this version and give me a new one to mess
with." Build your arrangement as a series of committed scenes, each a section or a
variation, rather than trying to plan the whole song up front.

## Scenes

FACT: A scene is a snapshot of which pattern is active in each of the four groups,
and a project holds up to 99 scenes. Switch scenes live by holding `MAIN` and
pressing `-` / `+`, or holding `MAIN` and typing the scene number. Switching is
instant and musical, so you can jump between sections while playing.

## Song mode (OS 2.0)

FACT: Song mode chains scenes into an arrangement of up to **99 positions**, each
holding a scene, for songs up to 9,801 bars. You build the chain in the song-list
editor (reachable by holding `MAIN` and pressing `ENTER`; this combo is from a
third-party manual, so **verify on device**). Each position plays for the length of
the longest pattern in its scene. Press `PLAY` to run the song; `SHIFT` + `PLAY`
starts from the top.

Assessment: a practical arrangement flow is: build 3-6 strong scenes (intro, verse,
chorus, breakdown, outro), then sequence them in song mode into the order you want.
Keep scenes slightly different from each other (a dropped element, an added fill) so
the arrangement breathes. If a transition needs something the scenes can't do,
perform a punch-in move and [resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) it into a
dedicated transition sample.
