# Chapter 18 — Starter project recipes

Six beats you can build start to finish, each in a different style. They exist to
turn the techniques from earlier chapters into muscle memory. Assessment: these are
creative starting points, not rules. The tempos and patterns are general
production conventions; the button steps trace back to the FACT chapters (sampling,
sequencing, FX), so follow the links when you need the mechanics.

## How to read a recipe

Every recipe uses the same layout:

- **Vibe / tempo / feel** so you know the target.
- **Group layout** following the course convention: `GROUP A` drums, `GROUP B`
  bass/808, `GROUP C` melody/chords, `GROUP D` texture/loops.
- **Patterns** written as steps on a 16-step bar (step 1 = the downbeat). To enter
  them, set the grid to `1/16` in `TIMING`, then either play live
  (`RECORD` then `PLAY`) or step-record (hold `RECORD`, move through steps with
  `-`/`+`, press the pad). See [chapter 8](08-sequencing.md).
- **FX / mix**, then a **"make it pro"** move (usually resampling or automation),
  then **arrange** (commit scenes).

A reading of "kick: 1, 7, 11" means put a kick on steps 1, 7, and 11 of the bar.

## Genre cheat sheet

Assessment, rough starting points:

| Style | Tempo | Feel | Signature element |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boom Bap | 85-92 | swung | chopped soul sample, cracky snare |
| House | 120-128 | straight | four-on-floor kick, offbeat open hat |
| Trap | 140 (half-time) | half-time | sliding 808, triplet hat rolls |
| Drum & Bass | 172-176 | breakbeat | two-step or Amen break, Reese/sub |
| Drill | 140-144 | half-time | gliding 808, offbeat snare, hat triplets |
| Lo-fi / ambient | 70-85 | swung, loose | sampled chords, vinyl crackle, wobble |
| Punk Rock | 180-200 | driving, straight | sampled power chords, distortion, stops |
| Grunge | 90-115 | loose, dynamic | loud-quiet-loud, resampled walls of fuzz |

Assessment: the fastest way to grow is to build them all even if a style isn't "yours."
Each one teaches a different core skill (chopping, four-on-floor groove, 808 glides,
breakbeat editing, syncopation, and texture/resampling), and those skills transfer.
