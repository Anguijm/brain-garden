# Chapter 23 — Recipe: Drill

Dark, menacing, and built around a **sliding 808** and a syncopated, half-time
groove. Drill shares trap's DNA but the 808 slides are more aggressive and the hats
and snares sit in more off-kilter places.

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **142 BPM**, half-time feel.
- Grid: `1/16`, with triplet rolls for hats.
- Key: minor and dark. Mood is the point.

## Group A — drums

Tight kick, snare/clap, crisp hat.

- **Kick:** step **1**, plus syncopated hits, try steps **7** and **11**, moved to
  taste. Drill kicks are restless.
- **Snare/clap:** the drill trademark is an **offbeat** snare. Put the main snare on
  step **9** (the "3"), then add a second, slightly early or late ghost snare (around
  step **11** or **12**) for that stumbling, off-grid feel.
- **Hats:** 16ths with frequent **triplet rolls** (note repeat at `1/16T`,
  [chapter 8](08-sequencing.md)) and sliding velocity.

## Group B — the sliding 808

The signature sound. Long 808 on `GROUP B`, pad in `legato` mode so notes glide, and
play it from `KEYS` so you can write a moving bassline. The drill move is to **slide
between two notes** (e.g. up a fifth then down), often landing the slide right on a
kick. Let the 808 be melodic and prominent; it is a lead as much as a bass.

## Group C — melody

A dark piano, bell, or string sample, minor key, sparse and eerie. Slide or pitch a
note for tension. Less is scarier.

## FX and mix

- **Sidechain** the 808 to the kick hard so the slides stay clean under the drums
  ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).
- Subtle **reverb** on the melody for space; keep the drums dry and upfront.
- Master compressor; drill wants tight, controlled low end, not boom.

## Make it pro

The sliding 808 is fiddly live, so [resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) your best
take to a pad and trigger it cleanly thereafter. Add a reversed cymbal or a riser
(perform a punch-in sweep and resample it) before each section change.

## Arrange

Keep sections sparse and let the 808 and hats carry the energy. Build the main loop,
commit it, make a tension scene (drop the drums, leave the melody and an 808 slide),
then drop back in. Chain in [song mode](12-arranging.md).
