# Chapter 9 — The fader and automation

The single fader is the K.O. II's main "movement" control. Learning to assign and
automate it is how your beats stop sounding static.

## What the fader controls

FACT: There is one physical `FADER`, and it is **assignable per group**. Hold the
`FADER` button and choose a destination from the options printed above the pads. It
can drive group level (the default), pitch, pan, attack/release, low-pass or
high-pass filter, time-stretch, and the FX-send amount. Each group remembers its own
fader assignment.

FACT: Although there is only one physical fader, a pattern can hold up to **12
recorded fader assignments**, so different parts of your beat can have different
parameters moving.

## Recording fader automation

FACT:

- **Live:** with `PLAY` running, hold `RECORD` and move the `FADER`. The motion is
  written into the pattern.
- **Per step:** while step-sequencing, hold `RECORD` and move the `FADER` to write a
  value to the current step.

The automation is **latching, not momentary**: a written value holds until the next
written value, so you can set a level at one point and a different level later and
the device ramps between your written points.

## Seeing and clearing automation

FACT: Hold the `FADER` button and any assignment that has recorded automation will
blink, so you can see what is moving. To clear it, hold `ERASE` + `FADER` for about
two seconds to reset the fader data for the group, or simply record new motion over
the old.

Assessment: the highest-value moves are a filter sweep on a bass or chord group to
open up a chorus, a level fade to bring a part in or out, and a pitch dip as a
transition. Because automation is per group (not per pad,
see [chapter 7](07-shaping-sounds.md)), organize sounds you want to move together
into the same group. When you need movement that the fader can't express per pad,
[resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) the moving version into a new sample.
