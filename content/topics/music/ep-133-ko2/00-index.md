# EP-133 K.O. II: a complete course

This is a beginner-to-advanced course for the Teenage Engineering EP-133 K.O. II,
the 128 MB sampler and beat composer (the bigger sibling of the pocket-sized
PO-33). It starts with what every button does and builds up to resampling,
song arrangement, MIDI, and sound-design tricks.

## How to read this, and how it's labeled

The vault rule is that every claim is FACT, Assessment, or Speculation. For this
course:

- **FACT** = how the device actually behaves, drawn from Teenage Engineering's
  official guide (teenage.engineering/guides/ep-133) and corroborated by published
  reviews. Specs and button functions are FACT.
- **Assessment** = technique, workflow advice, and "how to think about it"
  judgments. Useful, but my opinion, not the manual.
- A few details could not be pinned to the official guide; those are flagged
  **(verify on device)** so you don't take them as gospel.

Firmware note: this course is written for **OS 2.0 and later** (the "Champions"
update and its successors, current version 2.0.5). OS 2.0 added resampling, song
mode, sidechain, MIDI thru, hands-free sampling, and higher polyphony, so older
reviews that say "you can't resample" are out of date. Update your unit first
(see [chapter 15](15-samples-and-computer.md)).

## What the K.O. II is, in one paragraph

FACT: It is a standalone sampler, sequencer, and groovebox. You load or record
short samples onto 12 pressure- and velocity-sensitive pads, arrange them into
patterns, layer patterns into scenes, and chain scenes into songs. It has 999
sample slots, 128 MB of memory, a built-in mic and speaker, line and MIDI I/O, and
runs on USB-C power or four AAA batteries.
