# Chapter 24 — Recipe: Lo-fi / ambient sampling

The "sample anything, make it dreamy" recipe, in the spirit of the playful,
texture-first style Red Means Recording is known for. The beat is almost secondary;
the point is mood, found sound, and happy accidents from
[resampling](16-advanced-techniques.md).

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **78 BPM**, loose.
- Grid: `1/16`. Swing: push `knob Y` in `TIMING` fairly high. Lo-fi wants a lazy,
  behind-the-beat feel.

## Start by sampling something real

The lo-fi spirit is to sample the world, not a sound pack. Use the
[built-in mic or line in](05-sampling.md) to grab: a few seconds of a record or
YouTube jazz/soul, a piano chord, your own humming, rain, a kettle, room tone,
anything. Two or three short samples is plenty.

## Group C — chopped chords (the bed)

[Auto-chop](06-chopping.md) your musical sample across `GROUP C` and find a 2-4 chord
phrase by re-sequencing slices. Detune it slightly (lower the pitch a touch in
[sound edit](07-shaping-sounds.md)) for that woozy, tape-warped feel. Add a long
`attack` and `release` so chords swell instead of stabbing.

## Group A — soft drums

A soft, round kick, a rimshot or brushed snare, a gentle hat.

- **Kick:** steps **1, 11** (relaxed, not punchy).
- **Snare/rim:** steps **5, 13**, low velocity.
- **Hat:** swung off-8ths (steps **3, 7, 11, 15**), quiet and uneven on purpose.

Keep everything soft and a little sloppy; perfection kills the vibe.

## Group D — texture (the secret sauce)

This is where it comes alive. Load or sample **vinyl crackle**, tape hiss, rain, or
room tone and let it run under everything. Texture is what makes a simple loop feel
like a place.

## Group B — bass

A soft sub or upright on the root of your chords, `key` mode, sparse. One note per
chord is enough.

## FX and mix

- Roll the group **filter** down on the chords and drums so nothing is too bright
  ([chapter 10](10-effects.md)).
- **Reverb** on the chords and snare for space; a slow **chorus** adds wobble.
- Gentle **master compressor**, and a light **sidechain** of the pad to the kick for
  a subtle breathing pulse ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).

## Make it pro (the Red Means Recording move)

This is the fun part. Record a slow **pitch wobble** with the fader on the chord
group ([automation](09-fader-and-automation.md)) to fake tape warble, then
[resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) the whole loop, crackle and all, through the
filter and reverb into a single new sample. Now chop *that* and rearrange it for a
glitchy variation, or pitch it down for a darker section. Embrace the artifacts;
lo-fi is the one style where "broken" sounds better.

## Arrange

Lo-fi doesn't need much structure: a main loop, a stripped "just chords and crackle"
intro/outro, and maybe one variation with the drums dropped. Commit those as scenes,
keep it short and loopable, and let the texture carry it.
