# Chapter 26 — Recipe: Grunge (experimental)

Heavy, dynamic, and emotional, the loud-quiet-loud world of Pearl Jam and Smashing
Pumpkins. This is the most "band in a box" thing you can ask a sampler to do, so it
is experimental: the magic is in sampling grungy guitars and using **resampling to
build a wall of fuzz**, plus **scenes for dynamics**.

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **100 BPM** (grunge sits mid-tempo and heavy; try 90-115).
- Grid: `1/16`. Feel: looser and bigger than punk. Record drums in **free time** and
  only [timing-correct](08-sequencing.md) the kick and snare so it stays human.

## The guitars (the experimental core)

You want two textures: a **clean-ish** guitar for verses and a **thick, fuzzy**
distorted guitar for choruses. Sample both via the [line in](05-sampling.md) (a real
guitar through a fuzz/distortion pedal is ideal; drop-D power chords are the grunge
staple). Put chords on `GROUP C` in `KEYS` mode so you can play a slow, minor-leaning
progression.

- **Pearl Jam flavor:** keep it rawer and more dynamic, fewer layers, more feel; let
  notes ring and breathe.
- **Smashing Pumpkins flavor:** build a **wall of fuzz**. Record one distorted
  power-chord pass, [resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) it, layer a second slightly
  detuned pass on top, resample again, and repeat. Each resample stacks another
  guitar into the density. That layered, saturated wall is the Pumpkins sound.

## Group A — drums

Big, room-y kit: deep kick, a fat snare with reverb, crash, toms.

- **Kick:** steps **1, 9** (and a syncopated **11** in choruses).
- **Snare:** steps **5, 13**, big and a little reverberant.
- **Hat/ride:** 8ths in verses; switch to crash-heavy 8ths in the chorus.
- **Toms:** program a tumbling fill before each chorus.

## Group B — bass

Thick, slightly fuzzy bass following the guitar root, `key` mode. In quiet verses let
it carry the song almost alone.

## The dynamics (the soul of grunge)

Loud-quiet-loud is the whole genre. Build it with **scenes** ([chapter 12](12-arranging.md)):

- a **verse scene**: clean guitar low in the mix, soft drums, bass forward, sparse;
- a **chorus scene**: the distorted wall of guitar, loud drums, full energy.

Use the fader to automate a **level/filter swell** ([chapter 9](09-fader-and-automation.md))
building from the end of the verse into the chorus hit, so it explodes rather than
just switches.

## FX and mix

- **Distortion** on the chorus guitar group; a slow **chorus** effect adds the
  watery, early-90s shimmer (especially for the Pumpkins vibe)
  ([chapter 10](10-effects.md)).
- **Reverb** on the snare and a little on the guitars for size.
- **Master compressor** to glue the loud sections; ease off so the quiet parts stay
  quiet (don't over-compress away your dynamics).

## Make it pro

- The wall-of-fuzz resampling layering above is the signature move; 2-4 stacked,
  slightly detuned guitar resamples sound enormous.
- Sample **feedback or amp noise** and run it under section changes on `GROUP D` for
  authenticity.
- Pitch a guitar chord down for a heavier bridge.

## Arrange

Classic structure: clean intro → quiet verse → explosive chorus → verse → chorus →
big bridge/outro. Commit each as a scene and chain them in
[song mode](12-arranging.md). Resist filling the quiet parts; the silence is what
makes the loud parts hit.
