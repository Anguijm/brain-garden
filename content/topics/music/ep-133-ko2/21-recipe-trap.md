# Chapter 21 — Recipe: Trap

Half-time swagger, booming 808s, and skittering hi-hats. Trap is about space (the
half-time backbeat) and detail (the hat rolls and the 808 glide).

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: written as **140 BPM**, but it *feels* half-time (like 70). The snare
  landing once per bar instead of twice is what creates that lean-back feel.
- Grid: `1/16`, plus you will switch to a triplet grid for hat rolls.

## Group A — drums

Punchy kick, a snare or clap, a crisp closed hat.

- **Kick:** step **1**, then a syncopated second hit around step **11** (move it to
  taste). Trap kicks are sparse and off-grid.
- **Snare/clap:** step **9** only (the "3"). One backbeat per bar is the half-time
  signature.
- **Closed hat:** straight 16ths (every step) as the bed, then add detail below.

## Hi-hat rolls (the trap signature)

For rolls, use **note repeat**: hold `TIMING` and press the hat pad, and switch the
note interval to `1/16T` or `1/32` for bursts ([chapter 8](08-sequencing.md)).
Record a few fast rolls into the bar (a triplet roll before the snare, a 1/32 burst
at the end). Vary pressure for dynamics.

## Group B — the 808

This is the centerpiece. Put a long 808/sub on `GROUP B`, set the pad to `legato`
mode ([sound edit](07-shaping-sounds.md)) so notes **glide** into each other, and use
`KEYS` so different pads are different pitches. Write a melodic 808 line that follows
your melody's root notes; let some notes slide for that classic 808 glide. Keep the
808 and kick out of each other's way (sidechain below).

## Group C — melody

A dark, simple melodic loop: bells, a plucked lead, or a minor-key sample. Sparse and
moody beats busy.

## FX and mix

- **Sidechain** the 808 to the kick so they don't fight ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).
- Light **reverb** on the melody, **distortion** on the 808 if you want it to cut on
  small speakers.
- Master compressor to control the low end.

## Make it pro

[Resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) your 808 line (with its glide and saturation
baked in) to a single pad so it always plays consistently and frees voices. Add an
open-hat or crash on the first beat of each section for impact.

## Arrange

Trap arrangements breathe by subtraction: a full section, then a "drum-less" section
where only the melody and 808 play before the beat drops back in. Commit those as
scenes and chain them in [song mode](12-arranging.md).
