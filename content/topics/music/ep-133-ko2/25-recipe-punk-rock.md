# Chapter 25 — Recipe: Punk Rock (experimental)

Fast, loud, three chords and the truth. This is experimental on purpose: a groovebox
is not a band, so the goal is to bend it toward a punk record using sampled guitars,
the distortion FX, and relentless energy. How real it sounds depends mostly on the
guitar you feed it.

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **180 BPM** (push to 200 for skate punk).
- Grid: `1/16`. Feel: straight, driving, no swing. Energy over polish.

## The guitar (the experimental core)

Get a **distorted power chord** into the device. Best: plug a guitar through a
distortion/fuzz pedal into the [line in](05-sampling.md) and sample one palm-muted
power chord; or sample a power-chord from any source. Put it on `GROUP C` and use
`KEYS` mode so the 12 pads become different pitches, then play a simple **I-IV-V**
progression (e.g. E, A, B). For fast downstroke chugs, use **note repeat** (hold
`TIMING` + the chord pad) at `1/8` or `1/16` ([chapter 8](08-sequencing.md)).

## Group A — drums

Kick, snare, a ride/open hat, a crash.

- **Kick:** steps **1, 5, 9, 13** driving (add steps **7, 15** for a galloping
  double-time push).
- **Snare:** steps **5, 13** (beats 2 and 4), hit hard.
- **Ride/hat:** straight 8ths, steps **1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15**, full velocity.
- **Crash:** step **1** of each section.
- Record live and a little ahead of the beat (free time) for urgency.

## Group B — bass

A gritty bass on the root, **driving 8ths** locked to the guitar. Put it in `key`
mode and add a touch of distortion so it growls.

## FX and mix

- **Distortion** group FX on the guitar and bass groups ([chapter 10](10-effects.md));
  punk wants it raw.
- Almost no reverb; keep it dry and in your face.
- **Master compressor** (`SHIFT` + `FX`) pushed hard for that slammed, loud master
  ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).

## Make it pro

- [Resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) the full chord progression (distortion baked
  in) to a single pad so a whole bar of guitar plays from one hit, tight and
  consistent.
- Use a **mute group** ([chapter 7](07-shaping-sounds.md)) to get hard full-band
  **stops** (the classic punk stab-silence-stab).
- Sample yourself shouting "hey!" or "oi!" and drop gang-vocal stabs on the
  downbeats.

## Arrange

Punk songs are short and direct: a fast intro, verse, shouty chorus, repeat, and a
big stop ending. Keep sections to 4-8 bars, commit them as scenes, and chain them in
[song mode](12-arranging.md). Speed and a hard ending sell it.
