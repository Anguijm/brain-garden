# Chapter 13 — Performing live

The K.O. II is a capable live instrument. Performance is mostly the techniques you
already know, used in real time: switching patterns and scenes, muting, punching in
effects, and rolling fills.

## Muting with mute groups

FACT: There is no dedicated mute button. Muting is done with **mute groups** (set in
[sound edit](07-shaping-sounds.md)): pads in a mute group cut each other off, so only
the last one pressed sounds. The classic use is open and closed hi-hats choking each
other, but you can also use it to switch between variations of a part by tying them
together. (A separate simple per-track live mute is not documented; **verify on
device** if you need one.)

## Punch-in effects live

FACT: Hold `FX` and press pads to trigger the pressure-sensitive punch-in effects;
hold harder for more, stack several at once, release to snap back. These are your
main live drama: filter sweeps, stutters, and risers into a new section. Remember
they don't record into the pattern, so they are a pure performance layer
([chapter 10](10-effects.md)).

## Switching sections on the fly

FACT:

- **Patterns:** hold a group and press `-` / `+`, or hold the group and type a
  number, to flip that group's pattern while everything keeps playing.
- **Scenes:** hold `MAIN` and press `-` / `+`, or type the scene number, to jump
  whole sections.

Because each group switches patterns independently, you can build a performance by
swapping one group at a time (bring in the bass, then change the drums, then drop to
just chords).

## Fills and rolls

FACT: There is no one-button "fill." You make fills with **note repeat** (hold
`TIMING` + a pad for rolls and machine-gun hats, responsive to how hard you press)
and with **punch-in FX** (a stutter or beat-repeat). Together they cover the job.

Assessment: a simple, reliable live set is to lay out your sections as scenes, keep
your left hand on `FX`/`TIMING` for fills and effects, and use your right hand to
switch patterns and play accents. Rehearse the transitions, those are where live
sets live or die, and most K.O. II transitions are a punch-in riser plus a scene
change landing on the downbeat.
