# Chapter 11 — Mixing and the master

A good beat falls apart if the balance is off. The K.O. II keeps mixing simple:
levels and pan per sound and per group, plus a strong master section.

## Levels and pan

FACT: `MAIN` is the home / mixer view. Each sound has its own level and pan (set in
[Sound mode and sound edit](07-shaping-sounds.md)), and each group has its own
level, pan, pitch, filter, and FX send via the assignable `FADER`. By default the
fader controls the current group's level, so balancing groups against each other is
just selecting a group and moving the fader.

Assessment: mix in the order kick, then bass against the kick, then snare, then
everything else fits around those three. Pan hats and percussion a little off-center
to open space; keep kick and bass centered.

## The master compressor

FACT: There is a master compressor on the output. Enter the output settings with
`SHIFT` + `FX`; `knob X` is drive, `knob Y` is speed. Reviewers single this
compressor out as genuinely good, especially on drums, for adding punch and glue.

Assessment: a little goes a long way. Add drive until the beat feels more "together"
and louder, then back off before it starts to pump unpleasantly (unless pumping is
the vibe you want).

## Sidechain (OS 2.0)

FACT: The output also offers a sidechain compressor, the classic "ducking" effect
where one sound pushes another down (a kick ducking the bass for that pumping
groove). From `SHIFT` + `FX`, press `+` to switch to sidechain, choose the source
(hold a group pad and pick a sound), choose the destination groups (`SHIFT` + group
pads), and set length on `knob X` and shape on `knob Y`. Usefully, it can be
triggered by note events, so the source doesn't even have to be audible.

## Processing live input

FACT: External audio coming in the input jack can be run through the effects and
mixed in; set its input gain and send level from the `MAIN` view. That lets you play
a synth or another instrument through the K.O. II's FX in real time.

Assessment: the master compressor plus a touch of sidechain is most of what makes a
K.O. II beat sound finished. Get the static balance right with levels first, then
use the master section to glue, not to rescue a bad balance.
