# Chapter 22 — Recipe: Drum & Bass

Fast, rolling breakbeats over a deep sub. DnB is the best workout for your
[chopping](06-chopping.md) and [sequencing](08-sequencing.md) skills on this device.

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **174 BPM**.
- Grid: `1/16`. The energy comes from a busy, syncopated break, not from swing.

## The breakbeat (two routes)

**Route A — program a two-step (simplest):**

- **Kick:** steps **1, 11**.
- **Snare:** steps **5, 13** (beats 2 and 4).
- **Hats:** fast 16ths with velocity variation; add ghost snares between the main
  hits for the roll.

**Route B — chop a break (the authentic way):**
Sample a drum break (an "Amen"-style loop), `SHIFT` + `SAMPLE` to
[auto-chop](06-chopping.md) it across `GROUP A`, then **re-sequence the slices** into
a new order. Rearranging break slices is the entire DnB drum tradition; this is the
recipe that most rewards the device's chop tools.

## Group B — the bass

DnB is half drums, half bass. Put a deep **sub** on one pad (clean sine-like) for the
low end, and optionally a growlier **Reese**-style bass on another. Keep the sub
simple and long under the break; let it move with the root of your tune. `legato`
mode for glides.

## Group C — pads / atmosphere

A lush chord pad or a sampled atmosphere, sparse, to give the speed somewhere to
rest. Roll the group **filter** down so it sits behind the drums.

## FX and mix

- Keep the kick and sub locked; a touch of **sidechain** keeps the low end clean
  ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).
- **Reverb** on the snare for that big DnB crack.
- Master compressor to hold the fast transients together.

## Make it pro

[Resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) your chopped break to one pad once you love it,
then chop *that* again for double-time fills and edits. Layer a second snare an octave
up via pitch for extra snap.

## Arrange

DnB structure is intro → drop → breakdown → second drop. Build the full "drop" loop
first, then a stripped breakdown scene (sub + pad, drums out), then bring the break
back. Commit the scenes and chain them ([song mode](12-arranging.md)); a 16-bar
breakdown into a drop is the genre's signature move.
