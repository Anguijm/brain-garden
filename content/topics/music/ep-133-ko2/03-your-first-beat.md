# Chapter 3 — Your first beat

This is the quick win. By the end you will have a looping beat. Don't worry about
doing it "right"; the goal is to feel the loop close.

Assessment: the steps below are the standard first-beat flow; the button presses
themselves are FACT from the official guide.

## 1. Pick a project to work in

Hold `MAIN` and press a pad (1-9) to choose a project. Pick `6` for a clean, empty
slate, or pick `1` to poke at the demo content first.

## 2. Put some drums on the pads

Press `SOUND` to enter Sound mode, and make sure you are on `GROUP A` (press it).
Tap a pad, then use `-` / `+` to browse the library until you hear a kick you like;
it is now assigned to that pad. Move to another pad and find a snare, another for a
closed hi-hat. Three sounds is plenty to start.

Tip: the library is sorted, so kicks live near slot 1, snares near 100, hats near
200. Use `SHIFT` + `-`/`+` to jump ten slots at a time.

## 3. Set a tempo

Press `TEMPO` and turn `knob X` to set the BPM (try 90 for hip-hop, 120-128 for
house). You can also tap `TEMPO` in time to set it by feel.

## 4. Record the beat

There are two ways. Try the live one first:

- **Live:** press and release `RECORD`, then press `PLAY`. You get a four-beat
  count-in, then the pattern loops. Tap the kick on beats 1 and 3, the snare on 2
  and 4, and sprinkle hats. Whatever you play is captured into the loop, and you
  can keep layering as it goes around.
- **Step by step:** stop playback, then hold `RECORD` and press a pad to drop that
  sound on the current step; move through steps with `-` / `+`. This is the precise,
  unhurried way to place hits.

Made a mistake? Hold `ERASE` and press the pad whose hits you want gone.

## 5. Listen, then tidy

Press `PLAY` to start and stop. If your timing was loose and you want it tightened,
that is what quantize and "timing correct" are for (see
[chapter 8](08-sequencing.md)). If the loop feels stiff, add a little swing with
`knob Y` in the `TIMING` menu.

## 6. Keep it

Your work stays in the project automatically. When you want to start a second
section without losing this one, press `SHIFT` + `MAIN` to **commit** the current
loop as a scene and keep building (see [chapter 12](12-arranging.md)).

That's a beat. From here, the rest of the course fills in the depth: getting your
own sounds in by [sampling](05-sampling.md), [shaping them](07-shaping-sounds.md),
tighter [sequencing](08-sequencing.md), [effects](10-effects.md), and
[arrangement](12-arranging.md).
