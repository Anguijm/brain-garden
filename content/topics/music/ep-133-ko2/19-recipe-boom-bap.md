# Chapter 19 — Recipe: Boom Bap

Dusty, head-nodding, sample-based hip-hop. The whole genre is built on a chopped
loop, a hard kick and snare, and swing. This recipe leans on
[chopping](06-chopping.md) and [resampling](16-advanced-techniques.md).

Assessment throughout; button steps are FACT from the linked chapters.

## Vibe, tempo, feel

- Tempo: about **88 BPM** (`TEMPO`, `knob X`).
- Grid: `1/16` (`TIMING`, `knob X`).
- Swing: turn `knob Y` in `TIMING` up to a moderate amount. Swing is what makes it
  boom bap rather than a stiff loop; trust your ear.

## Group A — drums

Grab a fat kick, a cracky snare, a closed hat, an open hat (browse in `SOUND`).

- **Kick:** steps **1, 11** (and a quiet ghost on **8** if you want bounce). Keep it
  a little loose, not machine-perfect.
- **Snare:** steps **5, 13** (beats 2 and 4). This is the backbone.
- **Closed hat:** every off-8th, so steps **3, 7, 11, 15**, and let the swing pull
  them. Vary velocity by hitting the pads softer/harder when you record live.
- **Open hat:** step **15** occasionally for lift.

## Group C — the chop (the soul of it)

Sample a few seconds of a soulful loop (jazz piano, old vocal, strings) via the
[mic or line in](05-sampling.md), then `SHIFT` + `SAMPLE` to
[auto-chop](06-chopping.md) it across `GROUP C`. Re-sequence two or three slices into
a short phrase rather than playing them in order; that re-flip is the craft.

## Group B — bass

Put a simple sub or upright bass on `GROUP B`, set the pad to `key` mode
([sound edit](07-shaping-sounds.md)) so it gates, and follow the root of your chop on
the kick hits.

## FX and mix

- On `GROUP C`, use the group **filter** to roll off the highs for that dusty,
  behind-the-glass sound ([chapter 10](10-effects.md)).
- A short **reverb** or slap on the snare.
- **Master compressor** (`SHIFT` + `FX`) with a bit of drive to glue and add knock
  ([chapter 11](11-mixing-and-master.md)).
- `GROUP D`: add vinyl crackle/texture under everything.

## Make it pro

[Resample](16-advanced-techniques.md) the chopped phrase (with the filter baked in)
down to a single pad. That frees voices and locks the vibe. Bonus: nudge a couple of
hits off the grid ([nudge](08-sequencing.md)) so it breathes like an MPC.

## Arrange

Build a 4-bar loop, `SHIFT` + `MAIN` to commit it as a scene, then make a variation
(drop the sample for the intro, add an open-hat fill before the turn). Two or three
scenes is a whole beat.
