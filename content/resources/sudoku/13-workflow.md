# Lesson 13 — The solving order (your checklist when stuck)

Techniques matter less than knowing *which to reach for next*. Work cheap-to-expensive
and you'll rarely need the hard stuff. When you're stuck, walk this list top to
bottom; the first thing that works resets you to the top.

1. **Hidden singles.** Pick a digit, scan all boxes/rows/columns for its only home.
   This is the highest-value early scan.
2. **Naked singles.** Any cell down to one candidate? Fill it.
3. **Pencil in candidates** once placements slow down. You can't see the rest without
   them.
4. **Naked pairs/triples**, then **hidden pairs/triples.** Clear the clutter.
5. **Pointing and claiming** (locked candidates). The most reliable un-stickers.
6. **X-Wing.** First single-digit fish.
7. **Y-Wing / XYZ-Wing.** First three-digit move.
8. **Swordfish / Jellyfish.** Bigger fish, only when above stalls.
9. **Colouring**, then **XY-chains / remote pairs.** Chain logic.
10. **Uniqueness (UR, BUG).** Shortcuts when the pattern appears.
11. **Forcing chains / AIC**, and only then **guess and check.**

## Two habits that prevent most mistakes

- **Keep candidates honest.** When you place a digit, immediately erase it from every
  cell it sees. A stale pencil mark causes nearly every wrong deduction.
- **Re-scan after every placement.** One filled cell often unlocks a hidden single
  three steps away. Solving is a chain reaction; keep poking the top of the list.

## Difficulty, roughly

- Easy/Medium: singles and subsets alone.
- Hard: add intersections and X-Wing.
- Expert: add wings, fish, and chains.
- Diabolical: the deep end of Lesson 12, or uniqueness shortcuts.
